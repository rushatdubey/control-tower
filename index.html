Executive Decision Support

A distribution network fails one lane at a time, long before finance sees the bill.

Across 180,000 orders and six distribution centres, the network on-time rate looks healthy. It hides the fact that a single DC is quietly destroying the number, and by month-end the SLA penalties are already spent.

01 / THE BLIND SPOT

The average is healthy. The network is not.

The network delivers on-time-in-full 82% of the time, a respectable headline. But OTIF averages six very different operations. One DC runs at 91%, another at 68%. A single number lets a failing site hide inside a passing network until the cost lands on the P&L.

02 / THE REAL CULPRIT

It isn't slowness. It's unpredictability.

DC-3's average transit is barely worse than the network. What breaks it is variance: its delivery window swings twice as wide as any other site. A customer plans around a slow-but-steady lane. They cannot plan around one that is sometimes fast and sometimes very late.

Why variance, not the mean

DC-3's mean transit is 6.1 days versus 5.4 for the network, a 0.7-day gap that looks minor. But its standard deviation is 2.1x the network's. Cutting variance, not average speed, is the lever that moves its OTIF.

Executive Brief

SO WHAT

The one page that goes to the operations director: finding, cause, recommendation, expected impact.

Findings & recommendation

The network's 82% OTIF masks a structural outlier. DC-3 carries 2.1x the lead-time variance of the network and 41% of all exceptions on 17% of volume. The cause is concentrated: single-carrier dependency on its express lane, compounded by internal dispatch delays. Re-routing that lane and dual-sourcing the backup is projected to recover roughly six points of OTIF.

What we found
  • One DC drives 41% of exceptions on 17% of volume
  • Variance, not average speed, breaks its SLA
  • Three reason codes explain 79% of late deliveries
  • Service failure tracks the thinnest margin
What we recommend
  • Re-route DC-3 express lane to the reliable carrier
  • Dual-source the backup to remove single-point risk
  • Fix internal dispatch and order-hold delays
  • Monitor rolling-3M OTIF per DC, not the average
